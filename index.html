If you've driven on Indian highways, you've seen them — large cylindrical tanker trucks carrying cement in powder form. These are cement bulkers. And they're the backbone of bulk cement logistics. → Cement is loaded pneumatically at the plant → The bulker transports 25-30 tonnes per trip → At the site, compressed air pushes cement into silos → No manual handling, no spillage, no bags Also: V-shaped tanker trucks for specific discharge requirements. #CementBulker #Logistics #Infrastructure #BulkCement

A question I get asked often: "Why not just buy directly from the plant?" It's a fair question. If cement comes from a plant, why involve a distributor? Here's what most people don't realise: 1. Plants have minimum order requirements and scheduling constraints 2. Different projects need different grades — often from different plants 3. Supply disruptions require backup sources across multiple states 4. Someone needs to own the logistics end-to-end A distributor doesn't just "pass along" cement. A distributor absorbs complexity so the project team can focus on building. That's the value. It's not always visible, but it's always there. #CementIndustry #Distribution #SupplyChain #Construction
