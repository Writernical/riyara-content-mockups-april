Saravanan Udayar

Founder, Riyara Trading

Cement has an expiry date. And most people don’t know this. From the date of manufacture: → 0-3 months: Full strength. Ideal usage window. → 3-6 months: Slight strength reduction. Still usable. → 6+ months: Significant degradation. Should be retested before use. This is why bulk cement (used immediately upon delivery) often outperforms bagged cement that’s been sitting in a warehouse for months. Freshness isn’t just for food. It matters in construction too. #Cement #QualityControl #Construction #BuildingMaterials

Saravanan Udayar

Founder, Riyara Trading

Cement has organised manufacturers. Steel has national brands. But aggregates? Most of the aggregate supply chain in Tamil Nadu is unorganised. Small crushing units. Inconsistent quality. Unpredictable availability. No traceability. For a contractor managing a large project, this is a nightmare. You can have the best cement and the best steel, and still have problems if your aggregates are inconsistent. This is why we invested in building our own aggregate network — vetted crushing units, dedicated vehicles, quality checks. It’s the hardest part of our business to systematise. But it’s where the market needs it most. #Aggregates #SupplyChain #Construction #QualityControl

Three months ago, Riyara had virtually no social media presence. Today, here’s what I’ve learned from showing up consistently: 1. The construction industry is hungry for genuine content. Not ads. Not promotions. Real operational insights. 2. Educating the market builds more trust than any sales pitch. When we explained cement grades, TMT bar specifications, and aggregate grading, people actually saved and shared those posts. 3. The “don’t upset clients” constraint turned into our biggest advantage. By not promoting ourselves, we ended up building more credibility than any ad campaign could. 4. People want to hear from operators, not marketers. That’s the gap in this industry. Q3 starts now. Monsoon season. The real test of logistics capability. We’ll keep sharing what we see on the ground. #BuildingMaterials #SocialMedia #ContentStrategy #Entrepreneurship #Riyara
